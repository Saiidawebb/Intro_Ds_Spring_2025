plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))

plt.title("Victim Age vs. Time of Occurrence")
plt.xlabel("Time of Occurrence (Military Time)")
plt.ylabel("Victim Age")
plt.show()

The x-axis uses data time from the TIME OCC column which reflects military time. This means that midnight is shown as 0 and 11:00 PM is shown as 2300. This puts the range of the full day from 0 to 2300, as opposed to 12:00 AM to 11:59 PM. The scatter plot shows the relationship between Victim Age and time of occurrance. More simplified, it shows what age groups were more often victimized at different times of the day. While there appears to be a relativley weak correlation between age and time of crime occurrance, the outliers show trends. Older people over the age of 50 are more likely to be victimized between the range of the early morning and afternoon (7:00 AM to 3:00 PM). Young people under forty were generally vicitmized at similar rates throughout the day. There is a slightly higher concentration of crimes occurring later on in the day. The data appears in general chunks, with clusters appearing shortly after midnight, aorund midday, and towards the end of the night.¶
